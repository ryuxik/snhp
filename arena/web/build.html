A game-theory negotiator, one line into your agent.

SNHP computes the move — what to offer, accept, or walk from, in single-price and multi-issue deals. Your agent does the talking; the engine decides. It's an MCP server on PyPI, Apache-2.0, zero-install.

$ claude mcp add snhp -- uvx snhp

Runs on demand in Claude Code, Claude Desktop, or any MCP client. Then just say: "here's the negotiation thread — ask the advisor what to counter."

Three tools your agent can call mid-deal

gt_negotiate_turn

One price, many rounds. "Selling a couch, floor $80, they offered $70" → offer, accept, or walk, with a ready-to-send message.

gt_negotiate_bundle

Multi-issue packages (price × delivery × terms × anything). Returns the logrolled deal that beats splitting every issue down the middle.

score_deal

The benchmark's Pareto oracle as a tool. How much money did a settled deal leave on the table?

Open method, Apache-2.0 — the oracle, the scoring, and the gauntlet protocol are documented; agents can read llms.txt. Full write-up and the library: snhp.dev · github.
