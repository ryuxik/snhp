SNHP-NX/1 — package deals for agent payments

Agent-payment protocols are shaped like a checkout: a cart resolves to one price, and the buyer agent can pay or not. Quantity against delivery date, scope against price — there is no field for a trade like that. NX is an open extension that adds one. It mounts between the cart step and the payment step, and hands a settled package back.

OUR OWN PRE-REGISTERED KILL FIRED — READ THIS FIRST

We registered, in advance, that if cheap price-only haggling reached 80% of NX's deal-formation rate, the "you need bundling to close the deal" claim was dead. On a neutral mix of 240 procurement scenarios it reached 94.2%. So NX does not claim bundling is necessary for deals to happen. Most of the time a price alone closes it.

Mechanism, since it is legible: 226 of 240 scenarios already had a workable price range at the seller's quoted configuration. Registration: nx/PREREG.md. Result: nx/results/RESULTS.md.

WHAT SURVIVED

1. Package deals produce better deals. Same scenarios, same utility functions, share of the achievable joint surplus actually realized:

+3.9 points of the achievable pie over the best price-only arm. Price splits a pie; only a package changes its size.

2. When deadlines are tight, bundling does become load-bearing. Re-run on a high-urgency, tight-deadline regime, price-only recovers just 0.696 of NX's deal formation — roughly 30% of the workable trades are unreachable without moving more than price — and the surplus gap widens to +25.6 points (0.820 vs 0.564).

That second run is exploratory, not pre-registered, and self-labels as such when you run it. It locates a boundary; it does not establish a headline.

THE FOUR RULES AN IMPLEMENTATION MUST ENFORCE

I1 — never above list

No message may price a line above its listed price. Constructing one must raise, not be rejected downstream. The seller's opening quote is the sole list authority, so a buyer cannot invent headroom.

I2 — bounded

The round limit is declared when the session mounts. The proposal past it must raise. Nobody can be griefed into an endless negotiation.

I3 — whole packages only

Counters are complete packages, and accepting adopts the standing package verbatim. "Accept" can never smuggle in new terms.

I4 — receipt hook

Every settled session exposes a hash over the canonical transcript, plus an optional signature. Attestor-neutral: anyone may sign, under any scheme. The SNHP notary is one example, not a requirement.

WHAT IT DOES NOT DO

SCOPE, STATED UP FRONT
  • It moves no money and holds no goods. The host protocol settles the agreed package; NX just gets it agreed.
  • It does not police fairness. NX never verifies the seller's costs or the buyer's valuation. It bounds and records the negotiation.
  • Attestation is not settlement. It proves what was agreed and that nothing priced above list. It does not prove delivery.
  • All the measurements used scripted policies. They show what the message set can reach, not how clever an agent is. A smarter buyer still cannot express a quantity-for-date trade in a format that has no field for one.
  • Single seller, one line per item. Multi-seller packages and in-session re-quotes are open questions in the spec, not solved.
