WHY THIS IS DIFFERENT FROM A LOG FILE

A log says we recorded that we behaved. A receipt says here is evidence, checkable without us, produced by a system that could not have misbehaved in this specific way. That last part is mechanical, not a promise — the constructor raises rather than sign a price above list.

WHAT A RECEIPT ATTESTS

Never above list

The agreed price never exceeded the public list price. Enforced at three separate points — when the receipt is built, when it is issued, and again when anyone replays it.

The seller's floor didn't move

A probe tests whether the seller's reservation price shifted when the buyer's claimed outside option changed. If it moved, the receipt says so — plainly, in the record, rather than hiding it.

Same inputs, same price

The receipt carries a hash of the context and a one-way digest of the buyer's disclosure — enough to reproduce the price, never enough to recover what the buyer was privately willing to pay.

CHECK ONE WITHOUT TRUSTING US

The verifier is open and runs offline. Nothing here asks for the benefit of the doubt:

python -m core.notary verify receipts.jsonl

[OK] signature valid · never-above-list holds · chain intact
     signed by sha256:b518…  key_source=env

Tamper with any field and verification fails. Fetch the public key once from /v1/notary/key and you never need to contact us again — including years later, when the receipt matters and we might not be around.

WHAT IT DOES NOT ATTEST

THE LIMITS, STATED UP FRONT
  • Only what happened inside the mechanism. A deal negotiated somewhere else is not covered by a receipt from here.
  • Not that the buyer told the truth — unless an attestation token is attached. Without one, the receipt says so rather than assuming it.
  • Capacity venues are named, not papered over. When the seller's floor legitimately depends on scarcity, the receipt reports regime: capacity and marks that condition false. We would rather ship an honest false than a flattering true.
  • An ephemeral signature proves consistency, not identity. If a throwaway key signed it, the receipt says key_source: ephemeral and the verifier warns you.

WHO NEEDS ONE

  • You answer to an auditor or a regulator. Your agents set or accepted prices; "our system wouldn't do that" is not evidence. A receipt is.
  • You let software spend company money. The control your finance team wants isn't a dashboard — it's a record that holds up when someone asks.
  • You operate a venue agents transact on. A party to the transaction cannot be its own notary, the same way a bank cannot audit itself.
