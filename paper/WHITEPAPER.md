# When Does Negotiation Beat the Sticker?

## Brokered Nash Quoting as a Direct Revelation Mechanism: Where It Wins, Where It Ties, and Where It Must Be Verified

*SNHP Research — working paper, July 2026*

### Abstract

Posted prices are optimal for a committed seller who knows their demand curve (Riley & Zeckhauser, 1983). Real sellers routinely fail to price to their demand curves: the median U.S. supermarket chain forgoes an estimated $16M/year by pricing near-uniformly across stores *despite* observable store-level demand variation (DellaVigna & Gentzkow, 2019). We study **brokered Nash quoting** — a neutral mechanism that, at the moment of sale, computes a bilateral bargaining outcome over both sides' disclosures and returns a take-it-or-leave-it quote that never exceeds the seller's posted list. Formally it is a *direct revelation mechanism* `f: (disclosed WTP, outside option) ↦ (SKU, quantity, price)` built on three structural components: an *event-consistent disagreement point*, *shadow-priced inventory* with regime-consistent (censoring-aware) demand learning, and a *transaction-scaled minimum-gain buffer*. We place the mechanism relative to the Myerson–Satterthwaite frontier: it retains ex-post individual rationality and dyadic budget balance, so *where* two-sided private information holds it must trade efficiency against incentive-compatibility — but at finite-stock venues the seller's cost is known, the problem is one-sided screening, and M-S does not bind; the min-gain buffer is a chosen robustness reserve (analogous to a Chatterjee–Samuelson no-trade band), not an impossibility tax (§3).

Across nine venue families and ~40 pre-registered, paired, seeded simulations, we report an honest arc. **(1) Control.** At perfect calibration the mechanism *ties* a jointly-optimized, choice-model posted board on seller profit (a2a−posted +$0.12/+$0.04/day, CI includes 0) and *wins on consumer surplus* (+$0.81/+$0.66/day, CI excludes 0) — the durable edge is a welfare edge, not a profit edge. Against the single all-day price a real operator actually posts, it wins profit too (+$0.55/day [0.32, 0.78]), and against a typically mis-set sticker it wins +$2.60/day [1.81, 3.40]. **(2) Incentives.** At finite-stock venues a best-response search finds honesty at the buyer's optimum *without verification* — the disagreement structure removes the payoff to lies (a *weak* best response: in the safe region lies are un-rewarded, not punished; §3). We characterize when honesty is the buyer's best response and *prove* it for the single unit (`paper/THEOREM-IC.md`): it requires five conditions — discount-only shadow pricing, a report-independent seller reservation, an event-consistent disagreement point, a bounded/attested outside option, **and the min-gain buffer dominating the excess rent (`ℓ − c ≤ 2β`)** — not report-independence alone (§3). A sup-over-types battery confirms the tie is exact only up to that buffer: the worst type (a WTP-understater on a high-margin excess unit) still captures a *bounded* +$0.15–0.20/day the pooled mean hides (§5c). At soft-capacity venues (boba) the reservation is report-dependent, two-sided uncertainty returns, and misreporting pays +$1,099–1,171/day — there, attestation is a security requirement, not a discount tier. A second venue family (wholesale) confirms the boundary directly: report-independent tactics (anchoring, Boulware, positional play) are fully neutralized; only a bluff on a truly-private outside option survives, and only unattested. **(3) Deployment.** A behavioral extension with reference-price memory refutes the "surge value without surging" thesis (consumers react to the *level*, not to posted-vs-invisible) yet finds a fairness-safe both-win region; the shared-posterior network effect is pro-consumer under discount-only and collusive under rationing — a sharp, testable line. Every headline effect that shrank as we removed flaws that flattered the mechanism is reported with its superseded value intact.

### 1. Introduction

Agent-mediated commerce is arriving ahead of its price mechanism. Payment and checkout rails for autonomous agents are shipping across the industry, yet the step where a price is *formed* remains either a posted constant or an unconstrained model-vs-model negotiation. Both defaults have documented failure modes. Posted prices inherit their setter's errors, which are large and persistent in the field (DellaVigna & Gentzkow, 2019). Unconstrained agent negotiation is worse: frontier language models negotiating solo lose measurable money to naive fixed strategies, autonomous LLM pricers can drift toward collusive outcomes (Fish, Gonczarowski & Shorrer, 2024), and agent-to-agent bargaining is exploitable by adversarial counterparties by default (*The Automated but Risky Game*, 2025).

This paper evaluates a third design: a **neutral broker** that receives private disclosures from both sides — the buyer's willingness-to-pay and outside option, the seller's inventory state and costs — and posts a take-it-or-leave-it quote computed as a Nash bargaining outcome over the true joint frontier. The human (or agent) experience is a computed price at checkout, never above the displayed list; the negotiation is invisible. We treat the object as a *mechanism with evidence*, not a benchmark of model behavior. Every experiment is seeded, paired against its exact counterfactual, pre-registered in the repository before results were computed, and adversarially reviewed; several headline effects shrank or reversed as we removed flaws, and we report the survivors.

The paper answers four questions and, in each, reports where the answer is *no*:

1. **When does this beat the sticker?** Not at the knife edge: a well-calibrated, jointly-optimized posted board *ties* the mechanism on seller profit, exactly as Riley–Zeckhauser predicts (§5a). The durable, universal effect is a **consumer-surplus** edge; the seller-profit edge is real but scoped — against the price a real operator actually posts, and against miscalibration (§5b).
2. **Can it be gamed?** At finite-stock venues, no — honesty is the buyer's best response with no verification, and we locate *why* (§5c). At soft-capacity venues, yes, badly — and we locate *why* there too, and show attestation is then load-bearing.
3. **Where is the venue boundary?** Not per-venue but **per-line**: the lever we pick can be a venue's anti-lever. Posted clearance beats bilateral quoting where time is the only variable and buyers are interchangeable; bilateral quoting wins where buyer heterogeneity is the scarce information (§5e).
4. **Is it safe against human customers and at network scale?** Partly. The "surge value without surging" thesis is refuted; a mild-anchor both-win region survives; and the shared-demand-posterior network effect is pro-consumer *only* under a discount-only guardrail (§5d, §5f).

Our contribution is deliberately *constructive and empirical, not new bargaining theory*. The economics we build on are known — the outcome rule is Nash-in-Nash pricing (Collard-Wexler, Gowrisankaran & Lee, 2019); the event-consistent disagreement point is the outside-option principle (Binmore, Rubinstein & Wolinsky, 1986); the min-gain buffer is a Myerson (1981) reserve / no-trade band; the finite-stock-vs-capacity split is Myerson–Satterthwaite's own two-sided hypothesis; the setting (a posted ceiling with bilateral discount below over perishable inventory) is Kuo, Ahn & Aydın (2011); and the welfare result that the disclosure value accrues to consumers is the consumer-surplus vertex of Bergemann, Brooks & Morris (2015). What is new is the *assembly and the evidence*: (i) a single discount-only, identity-blind, type-enforced revelation rule that composes those primitives at the point of sale (§2); (ii) a single-unit characterization of *emergent* buyer-IC whose one novel element is the self-canceling disagreement coupling — routing the buyer's threat through the same disclosed valuation that enters the trade surplus — not the underlying outside-option principle (§3, `paper/THEOREM-IC.md`); (iii) the empirical demonstration that a *constructive, incentive-compatible* mechanism reaches the Bergemann–Brooks–Morris consumer-surplus vertex, verified in a pre-registered, adversarially-reviewed program across nine venue families (§4–§5); and (iv) two systems/positioning claims we find absent from prior art — a neutral, zero-cut *price-forming* broker for agent-to-agent commerce, and discount-only as the analytic line between a pro-consumer data cooperative and collusion — plus an honest catalog of boundaries (§7).

**What is new here — and what is not.** *Not new (all cited above):* the Nash-in-Nash outcome rule, the outside-option disagreement structure, the reserve/no-trade buffer, the M-S one-sided/two-sided split, the posted-ceiling-plus-haggle setting, the consumer-surplus welfare vertex (BBM 2015) and its discount-only *derivation* (Kuang et al. 2026). *New:* (1) the **assembly** — one discount-only, identity-blind, type-enforced revelation rule composing those primitives at the point of sale, with a pre-registered paired-counterfactual evidence program across nine venue families; (2) the **self-canceling disagreement coupling** — one disclosed valuation setting both the trade surplus and the buyer's threat (§3); (3) a neutral, zero-cut **price-forming** broker for agent-to-agent commerce, absent from every current protocol/benchmark (§6); (4) **discount-only as the analytic collusion line** — pro-consumer under a discount cap, collusive under rationing (§5f). *Genuinely open (the theory frontier we do not yet close):* whether the emergent-IC characterization survives **multi-issue** logrolling — the nonlinear-frontier, event-coupled-disagreement, continuum-type regime that lies inside van Damme & Lang's (2024) own stated open conjecture and is our actual go-to-market (§7–§8).

### 2. The mechanism

**Setting.** A seller holds finite, possibly perishable inventory with a posted list price per good — the *ceiling*: quotes never exceed it, and this is enforced in the protocol type, not by convention. Buyers arrive with private multi-good valuations, diminishing marginal utility over quantity, and a real outside option (a competitor's posted prices plus a walk cost). A quote is a bundle `(good, quantity, unit price)`.

**Brokered Nash quoting is a direct revelation mechanism.** Write the buyer's report as `θ̂ = (WTP disclosure, outside-option claim)` and the seller's observable state as `s = (inventory, costs, shelf life, learned demand)`. The broker computes

```
    f(θ̂, s) = argmax   g_s(bundle)^w · g_b(bundle)^(1−w)
             bundle∈B
             s.t.  g_s ≥ 0  ∧  g_b ≥ 0        (ex-post individual rationality)
                   price ≤ list                (discount-only, type-enforced)
                   g_s ≥ max($0.75, 0.15·list-value)   (min-gain buffer)
```

where `g_s, g_b` are the seller's and buyer's gains over the *disagreement point*, `B` is the enumerated bundle space, and `w ∈ [0.5, 1.0]` is a seller bargaining weight (`w = 0.5` is symmetric Nash; see §5b). We stress that "Nash bargaining" here is the **outcome rule** — the argmax of the weighted product of gains-from-trade above a disagreement point — not an appeal to the 1950 axioms. The mechanism's content is in how the three inputs to that rule are constructed:

1. **Event-consistent disagreement point.** The no-deal world is *one event* — the buyer's actual best alternative — and both threat points are priced off that same event. If the buyer's best alternative is the seller's own list board, the buyer's threat point is that surplus *and the seller's threat point is that margin*: a buyer the seller already had earns the seller no concession. If the best alternative is a competitor, the buyer's threat point is that surplus and the seller's is *zero* — a marginal customer is found money, and deep quantity deals to recruit them are rational. Pricing both threat points off one consistent event is, empirically, the single most consequential design choice (§5c); the naive version — independent threat points — loses catastrophically (≈−$23/day in development testing; an illustrative prototype measurement, not a committed standing artifact) by letting early bargain-hunters drain stock the lunch crowd would have bought at list.
2. **Shadow-priced inventory with regime-consistent demand learning.** Each quoted unit carries its opportunity cost: a unit expected to sell at list within the remaining horizon is worth its list margin to *keep* (selling it discounted displaces that sale); only genuinely excess or expiring units are cheap to move. Crucially, expected demand is learned from the seller's *own realized history under the mechanism* (an exponentially-weighted per-good level with a day-shock posterior, censoring-aware so stockouts do not read as low demand) — not from a formula fitted to the pre-mechanism world, which self-invalidates. This is a Lucas critique in miniature and recurs as the program's meta-pattern (§4): a shadow price computed from pre-mechanism demand shows *believed* gains (+$548/run) while realized profit falls (−$329/run).
3. **Transaction-scaled minimum-gain buffer.** The seller's believed gain must exceed `max($0.75, 15% of bundle list value)`. Believed gains carry forecast noise; without the buffer the mechanism leaks margin on near-zero-gain deals. The buffer is a policy parameter reported as a frontier, not a hidden constant (§5a) — and, as §3 makes precise, at finite-stock (one-sided) venues it is a chosen robustness reserve, not an impossibility tax; only at capacity venues, where M-S binds, does it also stand in for the efficiency tax.

The quote is delivered with a mandatory receipt (the "why"), an auditable context hash (same state + same disclosure → same price — an *identity-blindness* property as an artifact, not a promise: the quote is price-discriminating on disclosed WTP but blind to who the buyer is), and a TTL inventory hold. The quoting path takes *no buyer-identity parameter*; a quote above list or a quote without its receipt is unconstructible.

### 3. Theoretical placement: where SNHP sits on the Myerson–Satterthwaite frontier

The mechanism's structure is not incidental — it fixes its position among the impossibility results for bilateral trade.

**Where Myerson–Satterthwaite binds — and where it does not.** M-S (1983) shows that with *two-sided* private information, no mechanism is simultaneously efficient, interim-IR, budget-balanced, and incentive-compatible. SNHP is ex-post individually rational (`g_s ≥ 0 ∧ g_b ≥ 0`) and dyadically budget-balanced (the buyer pays the seller directly; the broker takes no cut), and ex-post IR implies interim IR while ex-post budget balance is the notion M-S uses — so *where the two-sided hypothesis holds*, SNHP genuinely sits inside the constrained class. But the hypothesis holds only at some venues, and this distinction is the whole point. **At finite-stock venues the seller's reservation is its known marginal cost / shadow salvage: the private information is one-sided (the buyer's valuation), the problem is one-sided screening, and M-S does not bind** — efficiency, IR, budget balance and IC are jointly attainable relative to the shadow cost. **At soft-capacity venues the seller's reservation depends on the buyer's own reported outside option (a freed slot is worth only its resale, which a claimed walk can talk down): two-sided uncertainty returns, M-S binds, and something must give.** The correct placement is therefore this *split*, not a blanket "SNHP sacrifices efficiency."

**The min-gain buffer is a chosen reserve, not an impossibility tax.** Believed gains carry forecast noise; the `max($0.75, 15% of list)` deadband is a deliberate Myerson-style reserve trading a slice of efficiency for robustness to that noise — *analogous* to a Chatterjee–Samuelson no-trade interval, though C-S's band is an endogenous equilibrium region from strategic misreporting whereas ours is an exogenous deadband. Its measured cost is small: at perfect calibration the mechanism concedes only a statistical tie to the strongest posted board (a2a−static profit −$0.05/day, CI [−0.25, 0.16]; 90-day `vend/results.json`). The qualitative point stands: a mechanism that *beat* the perfectly-calibrated sticker would be evidence of a bug — and once was (a pre-review irrational-acceptance rule, corrected under review).

**A characterization of emergent buyer incentive-compatibility.** The classical results (Crawford & Varian, 1979; Sobel, 1981) say misreporting pays in bargaining; we find honesty at the buyer's optimum at finite-stock venues, and locate exactly what makes it hold. A buyer's understatement can pay only by lowering the price, and for a fixed bundle the symmetric-Nash price `p* = c_eff + (V̂ − q·c_eff − d_b)/(2q)` is *decreasing in the buyer's own disagreement `d_b`* — a channel the report **can** move by inflating the claimed outside option, independent of the seller's reservation. Report-independence of the seller's reservation is thus **necessary but not sufficient**; the honest characterization requires five conditions (proved single-unit in `THEOREM-IC.md`):

> **Proposition (proved for the single-unit case in `paper/THEOREM-IC.md`; refined by the proof).** *Truthful disclosure maximizes the buyer's true surplus over the WTP-scaling × outside-option deviation class iff: (a) discount-only pricing off a shadow-priced margin, so that on non-excess (scarce) units any price below list is infeasible — the price is pinned at list regardless of report; (b) the seller's reservation on excess units is a report-independent function of state (`r = c_eff`); (c) an event-consistent disagreement point — the buyer's threat `d_b` derives from the same disclosed valuation that enters the trade surplus, so a price-lowering understatement lowers the buyer's own threat point by at least as much; (d) the buyer's outside option is bounded by a publicly-verifiable or attested value; **and (a′) the min-gain buffer dominates the excess rent: `ℓ − c_eff ≤ 2β`.** Each hypothesis is tight: drop (d) and a free-walk claim pays on excess-stock days; drop (a)/(b) and the soft-capacity (boba) exploit returns; drop (c) and the naive independent-disagreement version collapses (≈−$23/day, illustrative; §5c); drop (a′) — a high-rent or perishable excess unit — and a strong understatement collapses the buyer's own board threat, flips the no-deal event to "walk," and captures up to `ℓ − c_eff − β` of the excess rent.*

The proof (`THEOREM-IC.md`) shows the four conditions the earlier draft listed are **necessary but not jointly sufficient**: (b), (c), (d) bound the *outside-option* channel and (a) pins the *scarce* price, but none closes the *excess-unit WTP-understatement* channel — the buffer condition (a′) does. Where (a′) holds (scarce units always; low-rent excess units, `ℓ − c_eff ≤ 2β`) honesty is a *weak* best response — the buyer is indifferent (every report yields `v − ℓ`); lies are un-rewarded, not punished — where it fails (high-rent/perishable excess: sandwich, cola, candy at the vend catalog) a **bounded** leak survives (≤ `ℓ − c_eff − β` per unit, ≈ +$0.15–0.20/day at the sup over types, §5c), closed by a larger buffer or by WTP attestation (not outside-option attestation alone). The load-bearing protections are therefore the **discount-only shadow floor**, **event-consistency**, and the **buffer-vs-rent margin (a′)**. The characterization *organizes* the vend win, the boba failure, the naive-disagreement collapse, and the free-walk leak under one statement (THEOREM-IC §6) — their *directions* follow from it; their *magnitudes* are empirical, set by the type/state distribution, not implied by the theorem. It is now proven type-by-type for the single unit and confirmed by a sup-over-types + state-conditioned-adaptive battery on two venue families (§5c); the multi-unit/multi-good extension and colluding deviations remain open (§7). Attestation of the *value itself* pins the private-value counterfactual, enforcing honesty by verification (a verified-true disclosure leaves nothing to misreport, so buyer regret = 0 — enforcement by verification, not emergent IC; §5f).

### 4. Experimental method and rigor

**Worlds.** Each venue family is a discrete-event inventory/queue simulator calibrated to public benchmarks (Appendix; `CALIBRATION-TARGETS.md`). The flagship vending world models 7–8 goods with per-good list prices, unit costs, salvage values and shelf lives; hourly arrival and WTP curves (lunch-peak); nightly restock to par; mean-one lognormal day shocks; a weekly office calendar; occasional perishable oversupply; and a competitor ("bodega") posting its own demand-derived prices. Traffic is calibrated to 7–8 vends/day (SOTI/Cantaloupe 2025), with a labeled "Smart Store" P90 cell retained.

**Honest baselines.** The posted control is *profit*-optimal, not revenue-optimal. Where a referee challenge exposed a weak baseline, we strengthened it and re-ran everything: the headline posted arm is now `posted` = a **discrete-choice-model board, jointly optimized by coordinate ascent** over the whole SKU set from a synthetic WTP panel, using the *identical* demand call the mechanism makes (`StrongPostedPolicy`). We additionally report `static` = the profit-optimal single all-day price (what a real machine posts), and `gvr` = a Gallego–van Ryzin bid-price dynamic arm with the same learner.

**Information discipline in code.** World truth and operator knowledge are separate values in separate fields (`WTP_MU` vs `Listing.wtp_mu_est`); a policy that touches ground truth raises. Dynamic arms observe only what a physical machine could: arrivals and their own sales. Seller miscalibration `σ_cal` scales lognormal error on the demand-level estimate that sets both the sticker and the arms' structural beliefs.

**Statistics.** Every arm faces the identical customer stream: randomness derives from `blake2b(seed, domain-tag, identity)` substreams keyed on *who arrives when* and *who a liar is* — never on anything a policy did. Effects are paired daily (or weekly) differences; because learner state and inventory carry across days, confidence intervals use block means (5-day blocks; 7-day for weekly-repricing fashion). **No win is claimed when the 95% CI includes zero.** The headline profit/consumer-surplus *tables* (the §5a strong-posted table, the §5b seller-weight frontier) are 90-day (`vend/tilt.json`) or multi-seed; the §5a perfect-calibration control (`vend/results.json`) and the §5c liar sweep (`vend/liar-sweep.json`) are also 90-day, and the only remaining 30-day cell is the §5b LLM contrast (`vend/h4-llm.json`, a paid-LLM run held at 30 days), labeled as such.

#### 4.1 Rigor standards — why an independent reader should trust this

1. **Pre-registration in the artifact.** Hypotheses, expected signs, and grids are committed to design documents *before* results exist. Where a result contradicted the registration, the contradiction is the reported finding (`paper/CRITICAL-ANALYSIS.md` records dated verdicts for §5a–§5f).
2. **Paired counterfactuals as a tested property.** Byte-identical customer streams are a unit test (`test_arrival_and_consumer_streams_are_policy_independent`), as is full-run determinism (two runs → identical result dictionaries).
3. **Invariants in types, not documentation.** A quote above list, a quote without a receipt, and a buyer-identity-dependent quote are each unconstructible; every quote carries a context hash making "same context + same disclosure → same price" auditable from artifacts alone.
4. **Strongest-baseline discipline.** When a ceiling search revealed our "optimal" sticker undervalued the seller's local monopoly power (+$21/day available by anchor placement alone), and when a referee argued the per-SKU dynamic arm was a strawman, we built the choice-model jointly-optimized board and re-ran everything. The mechanism's reported profit result is the *tie* against that stronger board (§5a).
5. **Adversarial review with consequences.** An independent multi-angle review of this codebase surfaced ~18 defects, of which two were material simulation biases (both flattering the mechanism). All were fixed and every artifact regenerated; **both material fixes strengthened the surviving result** (attestation now mechanism-enforces honesty rather than assuming it; the strong-posted baseline made the welfare cut cleaner). Superseded numbers remain in each `RESULTS.md`. A result that only survives its friendliest implementation is not a result.
6. **Conservation and accounting tests.** Money and units are conserved across every ledger (consumer spend = venue revenue; units vended ≤ units stocked; the three-tier supply chain conserves gains, `wholesale/results-supply.json`); profit identities are pinned to rounding tolerance.
7. **Tuned parameters disclosed as frontiers.** The gain buffer and the seller weight `w` are policy parameters reported as full trade-off curves with the in-sample cell named and held-out seeds validated (§5a–§5b).
8. **Information discipline in code** (as above): a policy touching ground truth raises.
9. **One-command reproduction.** Every table regenerates from a fresh clone via Appendix A; committed JSON is byte-comparable against reruns and a regression test fails on drift.

**The meta-pattern (the design rule that fell out of the failures).** Every real defect we found was the same defect: *some term in the machine's objective still assumed the pre-mechanism world* — demand forecasts (fixed via own-history learning), displacement shadows (fixed via censoring), capacity relief (fixed via nego-regime relief basis), and the baseline's own crowd model (fixed by the strong posted board). The rule: **every dollar in the broker's objective must be measured in the regime the broker creates.** Where we applied it, the mechanism wins or correctly ties; where we had not yet, it lost.

### 5. Results

We organize around the honest arc rather than by venue.

#### 5a. Control — a2a ties a competent posted price; the durable edge is welfare

Against the profit-optimal sticker at *perfect* calibration in a stationary world, the mechanism is a statistical tie on profit and a win on consumer surplus (`vend/results.json`): a2a−static profit **−$0.05/day [−0.25, 0.16]**, consumer surplus **+$0.97/day [0.43, 1.51]**; the per-SKU dynamic arm loses (gvr−static −$2.20/day [−2.60, −1.80]) (90 paired days). This *replicates Riley–Zeckhauser*.

Against the strongest posted baseline — the choice-model, jointly-optimized board with the same demand information — the tie holds and sharpens into a welfare result. In the realistic calibrated cell (90 paired days, 7–8 vends/day, ±30% miscalibration, day shocks, calendar, oversupply; symmetric `w = 0.5`; `vend/tilt.json`):

| a2a − posted (strong board) | seed A | seed 7 | pooled (block CI) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **seller profit / day** | **+$0.12** | **+$0.04** | +$0.08 [−0.12, 0.28] — *tie* |
| **consumer surplus / day** | **+$0.81** | **+$0.66** | +$0.73 [0.40, 1.07] — *win* |

The interpretation is information-theoretic: the posted board has *less* information per transaction (a crowd belief, no individual wallet) and still ties on profit, so the mechanism's disclosure advantage necessarily lands on the side the disclosure is *about* — the buyer. **Disclosure's realized value at a well-calibrated committed-seller venue is a welfare edge — more buyer surplus at equal seller profit — not a seller-profit edge.** This welfare geometry is *not itself new*: it is the consumer-surplus-maximizing vertex of Bergemann, Brooks & Morris (2015), who prove that a richer-information pricing mechanism can hold the seller at its uniform-price profit while routing the residual of efficient trade to consumers. Nor is the *constructive* route new: Kuang, Li, Liu & Yu (2026) prove that in *any* strategy-proof (voluntary, consumer-IC) market segmentation, producer surplus is pinned at the uniform-monopoly profit, consumer surplus ranges over [uniform, buyer-optimal], and — the point for us — **no consumer ever faces a price above the uniform-monopoly price**, so *discount-only is derived as a theorem, not assumed*. SNHP is therefore best read as a *specific individualized Nash-bargaining implementation* of that characterization at the point of sale — reaching a point on the Kuang/BBM frontier via voluntary disclosure and an event-consistent disagreement rule — not a new welfare result. This *strengthens* the positioning rather than weakening it: discount-only becomes the provable signature of decentralized consumer self-protection, a citable theorem rather than a design choice. It is corroborated by the LLM-priced control (§5b) and the buyer's-agent subsystem (§5f).

The min-gain buffer's trade-off frontier is documented rather than hidden: a flatter buffer converts the perfect-calibration concession toward zero at the cost of the fairness protection in §5d; the full curve, in-sample tuning cell, and held-out validation are in `vend/RESULTS.md`.

#### 5b. The miscalibration channel and the seller-weight frontier

Real sellers routinely misprice — from a noisy demand estimate (the small operator) or from not acting on known store-level heterogeneity (the chain; DellaVigna & Gentzkow, 2019) — and the mechanism's *profit* value lives precisely there. Against the single all-day price a real operator posts, the mechanism wins on profit — **+$0.55/day [0.32, 0.78]** (`vend/tilt.json`, a2a−static at the calibrated cell) — and against a typically mis-set sticker, **+$2.60/day [1.81, 3.40]** at the realistic-miscalibration cell (`vend/grid.json`, `cal0.3_shock0.6`). The gap between "ties the hypothetical hourly-repricing board" and "beats the single price you actually post" is exactly the value of hourly repricing; the mechanism captures both time-of-day variation *and* within-hour heterogeneity over a single price, whereas even a competent hourly board closes only the first. The irreducible residual over even the hourly board is within-hour heterogeneity, which no posted price can touch — which is why it surfaces as consumer surplus (§5a). Per-machine dollars are thin by construction (7–8 vends/day; one seed's CI touches zero); the commercial unit is the route (operators run 50–200 machines), on the order of +$26–106/day/route (scaled from the per-machine deltas, not directly simulated).

**The seller-weight frontier (`vend/tilt.json`).** We generalize the symmetric rule to asymmetric Nash, maximizing `g_s^w · g_b^(1−w)` above the disagreement floor (`w = 0.5` byte-identical to the symmetric mechanism), and sweep `w ∈ [0.5, 1.0]` at the realistic cell. Three break-points map cleanly and are the finding:

1. **Consumer surplus never crosses zero** anywhere in `[0.5, 1.0]`. Even at full seller-take (`w = 1.0`), buyers stay strictly ahead of the strong posted board (cs−posted +$0.45/day [0.01, 0.89]). The disagreement floor plus pie-growth structurally prevent the mechanism from becoming a pure extraction tool.
2. **Incentive-compatibility breaks at `w = 0.7`.** WTP understatement flips from not-paying (`w ≤ 0.6`) to the buyer's best response (`w ≥ 0.7`). A seller-favoring mechanism destroys the disclosure it runs on.
3. **Paper profit rises monotonically to +$1.37/day at `w = 1.0`, but the *defensible* profit peaks earlier and lower.** The maximum tilt that keeps disclosure both **honest** (IC intact) and **fair** (CS positive) is `w = 0.6`: profit−posted **+$0.35/day [0.13, 0.57]** (~+3% over the strong board, more over a real sticker), with consumers still **+$0.99/day [0.59, 1.38]** ahead. (An earlier draft reported +$0.61/day at `w = 0.7`; a review fix that banks the honest number via attestation moved the defensible optimum down to +$0.35/day at `w = 0.6`, and the superseded value is preserved — this is a representative "honest shrink.")

The frontier is itself the safety argument: past `w ≈ 0.7` a seller-favoring mechanism is self-limiting because it starts to be lied to; and CS never goes negative, so the mechanism structurally cannot become an extraction tool (contrast RealPage, §5f).

**The LLM contrast (`vend/h4-llm.json`).** Replacing the engine with an LLM (haiku) that talks *and decides* prices beats the sticker by less than a dollar (+$0.87/day) and returns customers **+$0.00** extra surplus — versus the engine's +$2.42/day profit and **+$9.46/day** consumer surplus. Removing the LLM from price formation is what produces the two-sided welfare.

#### 5c. Emergent incentive-compatibility, and its boundary (the theorem, demonstrated)

**Finite stock: honesty is the buyer's best response *on average* with no verification.** In the vending liar battery (`vend/liar-sweep.json`; liar identity keyed on `uid`, never on policy; 25/50/100% liar shares understating WTP and claiming a free outside option), no liar cohort significantly gains *in the population mean*: consumer-surplus deltas vs the honest population are −$0.10/day [−0.23, 0.03] (25%), −$0.15/day [−0.46, 0.17] (50%), −$0.30/day [−0.59, −0.02] (100%) at 90 days; every profit delta includes zero, and the 100% cohort ends marginally *worse* than the honest population — the exploit is absent, not merely small. The reason is structural and is the Proposition of §3: understating collapses the broker's estimate of the buyer's *board-purchase* alternative, which zeroes the seller's obligation to that buyer — on scarce units the deals a liar denies themselves exceed the discounts they extract, and the scaled buffer removes the thin-gain region where anchoring used to profit.

**The sup over types finds the residual the mean hides (Task #68B, the referee's ask).** A pooled mean washes out a profitable minority. A unilateral deviation probe that holds the world honest and reports the *worst type's* best-response gain (`vend/battery.py`, 6 seeds × 180 days after a learner burn-in, attestation off) shows the pooled tie conceals a small, real, **buffered** leak: on the excess (high-margin) stratum the plain WTP understatement pays **+$0.17/day [0.09, 0.25]** (the sup over types; the excess×high-outside *intersection* is smaller, +$0.08/day [0.02, 0.14]; replicated at +$0.15 with a high-glut stress cell and with an *oracle-excess* attacker who targets exactly the shadow-excess SKUs). This is precisely the §3 (a′) prediction — a bounded excess-unit rent capture on the high-margin/perishable SKUs (`ℓ − c_eff > 2β`), absent on the cheap ones — and it is why the mechanism is a Bayes-Nash claim *up to the buffer*, not an exact one. Three honest qualifications match the proof: (i) the **state-conditioned adaptive liar** (understate only where visible stock is high — the decisive untested deviation) and the **per-SKU favorite** attack do *not* beat the uniform lie; their sup is non-significant, so honesty survives the harder deviations without a *new* leak. (ii) The committed sweep's 80%-power **MDE was $1.25/day** (30 days, 1 seed) — it could not have seen a $0.15–0.30 exploit; the battery's MDE is $0.11/day, which is why the leak is now visible. (iii) A learner *converged on the liar population* opens no leak a cold learner hid (warm ≈ cold). The dominant residual remains the **free-outside-option claim** (+$0.49/day sup, all strata), which attestation banks; the WTP residual is closed by a larger buffer or WTP attestation. Verification is thus a *discount tier* for the headline (pooled) honesty and a *bounded-leak closer* for the excess type — not a security requirement, as it is at capacity venues.

**Soft capacity: honesty is not a best response, and attestation is a security requirement.** At boba (a queue/capacity world; `boba/liar-sweep.json`) the picture inverts exactly as §3 predicts. Understating WTP (~0.7×) while claiming a strong outside option flips the shop's disagreement branch: it believes it has nothing to lose even facing a genuine buyer. The best-response deviation nets buyers **+$1,099–1,171/day pooled** (tight CIs, both seeds); erosion is near-linear in liar share; **25% liars already cost the shop −$257 to −$274/day**, and **~33–36% liars wipe out the entire +$270–350/day cart headline**. The natural capacity fix does *not* rescue it: an observable-market floor (cap a claimed outside option at what the buyer's own disclosed valuation earns at the *visible* competitor board — using rivals' public prices only to *validate* a claim, never to set ours, distinct from RealPage) provably removes the entire observable lie but only cuts the exploit to **+$797–850/day** (`boba/liar-sweep-floored.json`), because the buyer just understates the genuinely-private WTP harder — the term the floor cannot reach. Pinning the outside-option term is necessary but insufficient; at a capacity venue the private-value term itself is manipulable in a way finite stock is not. The honest resolutions are (i) attestation as a security requirement, or (ii) a liar-immune public menu, which is structurally safe but keeps only ~10% of the discriminating gain (`boba/menu-fairness.json`: menu margin +$29.62/day [−2.48, 61.71], a tie, at consumer surplus +$405/day).

**Second venue family confirms the boundary (`wholesale/results-human.json`).** Modeling the pre-SNHP baseline as humans negotiating *badly* — eight literature-grounded types (hardballer, accommodator, bluffer, avoider, Boulware, positional, nibbler, fair), whose tactics *are* the misreport strategies — the SNHP split is byte-identical across all 64 type-pairs, and every **report-independent** tactic (anchoring, Boulware, positional, information-asymmetry exploitation) is fully neutralized because the supplier's reservation is pinned by COGS and finite stock. The **only** report that survives is the **bluff on a truly-private outside option, and only in the unattested regime** (the wholesale forecast is attested at settlement). This is precisely the §3 boundary observed across an independent second venue family — strong evidence the abstraction is right.

#### 5d. The behavioral / fairness extension (transitional — human-in-the-loop, not the A2A endgame)

We extend the vending world with persistent repeat customers carrying per-good reference prices (EWMA of prices paid, weaker update from prices observed), loss-averse transaction utility, sticker shock, dissatisfaction with symmetric relief, permanent churn, and exogenous pool replenishment (parameters literature-anchored: λ ∈ [1.66, 1.95], reference carryover 0.47–0.85; `CALIBRATION-TARGETS.md §5`). We scope this as *transitional* — it models a human in the loop, not the agent endgame.

**The "surge value without surging" thesis is partially refuted (`vend/surge.json`).** The hypothesis was that a broker could harvest peak value invisibly (a computed quote, no visible surge) where an explicit surge would churn customers. It fails on its premise: consumers react to the **price level**, not to posted-vs-invisible. At a ×1.25 peak anchor, both an explicit surge and the engine harvest large short-run value (+$29.76 and +$36.68/day net vs static) but both destroy consumer surplus (−$12.15 and −$15.39/day) and churn regulars (44–73 of 120) — fairness is **load-bearing and binds on both arms**. What *does* survive is the mild-anchor both-win: at the everyday reference (×1.0), the engine captures **+$1.35/day net over an explicit surge** while returning **+$0.61/day more consumer surplus** and healing churn via below-reference deals — the protective channel (quotes fire widely for small baskets near reference; the salient price is the customer's quote, not the board) is real but only inside the fairness-respecting band, not above it.

**The agent-mediated regime grows the edge (`buyer/results.json`, `regime` cell).** As friction → 0 and churn accelerates in the agent world, the mechanism's advantage over posted *grows*: buyer regret falls ~63% relative to the human-friction regime. The mechanism's edge is largest in the world it helps create.

#### 5e. The venue boundaries — claims are per-line, not per-venue

The general lesson from modeling ten venues: *the single lever we happened to pick can be a venue's anti-lever; the honest claim is per-line.*

- **Florist (`bakeshop/services.json`).** Modeled as pure perishable walk-in clearance, the florist is an anti-lever: a posted markdown board beats bilateral splitting where time is the only variable and buyers are interchangeable (posted keeps the clearance slice, bilateral concedes −$83.07/day). But clearance is only ~20% of a real florist's revenue. Adding the services a florist actually runs on — arrangement (grade×style logroll), events (scope×palette), delivery (route density), attach (complements) — flips the P&L: posted-only $1,332.70/day → deployable broker (bilateral + menu fallback) $1,671.09/day = **+$338.39/day (+25.4%)**. Per line, `nego − posted`: arrangement **+$165.52**, event **+$163.84**, attach **+$54.42**, delivery **+$37.68** (all CIs clear of zero), clearance **−$83.07** (conceded). The harder falsification — *standalone* bilateral vs posted — splits informatively: arrangement wins outright (**+$26.12/day [10.71, 41.53]**, genuine multi-issue taste; a test pins the premium-blooms-in-cheap-wrap logroll), events are horizon-sensitive (−$148.48, CI includes 0), and delivery (−$46.83) and attach (−$137.52) *lose* standalone because they are already efficiently postable — bilateral adds only a bounded heterogeneity top-up that pays *kept on the posted base*. **Corrected boundary: posted wins where time is the scarce variable; bilateral wins where buyer heterogeneity is the scarce information — not "florists don't benefit."**
- **Slots — no-shift beats full negotiation at short-peak walk-in venues (`slots/results.json`).** After correcting a calendar-blind relief learner (which had spuriously deepened the loss to −$400/day by valuing busy weekend-afternoon slots at near zero), the verdict is clean: at the bar, `nego-noshift` (+$245.60–276.71/day vs static) beats full `nego` (+$139.53–216.72/day) by a trustworthy **~$56–106/day**. Whether a freed peak slot is locally rebooked within a ±30-min window is same-day-trajectory state no day-level learned slot value can carry — which is exactly why slot-shifting logrolls are *boba-shaped* (order-ahead venues have that trajectory; walk-in venues do not). Parking negotiation survives the elasticity recalibration (+$111–178/day; commuters confirmed least-elastic, |e|≈0.81); the barber is ≈0 as modeled, correctly (deposits are the venue's *incumbent* mechanism, §7).
- **Vintage — broadcast retag is an offer-complement, not a standalone board (`vintage/results-v4.json`).** Bidirectional retagging (the hazard learner may re-tag up on high-connection one-of-one items, where no reference price exists to protect) initially looked like a large win, then reversed under realistic weeks-to-months resale hazard (ThredUp ~50%/30 days). A referee argued the reversal was a tuning artifact (the PV-solve's holding cost was tuned to a fast-sale world). Retuning to the real timeline confirmed the challenge was *half* right: the −$268–654 (per 60-day run) magnitude was mis-tuning (it zeroes to a wash once the holding belief is corrected), but the direction hardens — **broadcast retag tunes to a wash at best and never wins**, while the per-counterparty offer arm, carrying the identical engine, is never a significant loss (large wins at generous shading). The durable finding is the broadcast-vs-targeted asymmetry: a targeted quote is declined by one buyer, a broadcast markdown bleeds to everyone.
- **Fashion — markdown beats the cliff, and simple beats estimate-hungry on thin data (`fashion/results-v4.json`).** Weekly computed markdowns beat the industry's fixed-cliff calendar in every cell and *strengthen* under 17–26% returns: markdown−cliff **+$1,666/season** (0% returns) → **+$3,211** (17%) → **+$3,664** (26%), all CIs excluding zero (consistent with Caro–Gallien 2012). And the boundary on "optimize everything": a learned-demand, returns-aware optimizer (`opt`) *loses* to the fixed markdown in every cell (point-negative in all 27; significantly in 22/27; −$397 to −$2,709/season) because margin is concave in an ~8%-noisy estimate on a single-buy 16-week season; the returns-timing-only arm is a wash. On thin-data single-buy seasons, a simple robust schedule beats a cleverer estimate-hungry one.
- **Boba — capacity-smoothing is the biggest lever, and it survives the functional-form fix (`boba/balk-respec.json`).** Re-specifying abandonment as nonlinear in queue *length* (Lu et al. 2013) rather than expected wait, the off-peak-smoothing lever survives and grows: **+$212–256/day** (flex 0.15→0.35), all CIs excluding zero. This is the pro-consumer lever (fewer peak balks, less waste) and is fairness-clean — distinct from the exploitable discriminating cart of §5c. A separate **pre-checkout suggestion** arm (`boba/results-suggest.json`) shows *when to offer* matters more than *whether to*: a learned policy that suggests one discount-only upgrade only in the right observable state beats **both** never-suggest (+$9/day, CI excludes 0) and always-suggest (always-suggest itself *loses* −$65/day by asking everyone and eating the annoyance hazard), with the "when" boundary *inverting* the naive upsell — offer one-more-cup to an off-peak group that already bought pearls, hold otherwise. Honestly contingent on the annoyance rate, which is swept.
- **Bakery (`bakeshop/results.json`).** Bilateral quoting beats the control **+$166.18/day [131.37, 200.99]** at +$119.73/day consumer surplus and −$80.31/day waste; the folk noon day-old pull (a cannibalization control) beats naive dynamic tiers, evidence that cultural pricing practices encode real constraints a controller must implement rather than caricature.

#### 5f. Both sides, and networks

**The buyer's agent (`buyer/results.json`).** Pointing the same mechanism at the buyer, a disclosure-driven agent beats a naive buyer by **+$0.59/deal** (regret 2.05 → 1.46) unattested; **under full (WTP + outside) attestation, buyer regret → $0.00** — enforcement by verification (a verified-true disclosure leaves nothing to misreport), the §3 attestation case realized on the buy side, not emergent IC. Decomposing where the value comes from: a one-shot **shop** across `m = 3` merchants is a pure *transfer* (+$0.93/deal buyer surplus, joint value unchanged, CI includes zero), while **coordinate/commit** over time *creates* value (joint +$0.57/deal, significant; proven-trust portability +$0.71/deal). Value in commerce comes from coordination and commitment, not from out-haggling a counterparty.

**The supply-side mirror (`wholesale/results-supply.json`).** Procurement is the buyer's agent pointed upstream: **one mechanism operating twice** on a three-tier chain (consumer→merchant→supplier). The chain conserves gains exactly (`S3_decomposition.conserves = true`); attestation ("engine certainty") adds **+$182.51/week** joint by removing spoilage-risk padding; and a monopsony audit **passes at both interfaces** (coordination never prices below independent, the participation floor holds, and overreach is self-defeating — `S4_auditA`, `S4_auditB` all true). Neutrality survives being run on both sides of the same merchant.

**The network effect and the collusion audit (`block/results-b6.json`).** Adopters sharing a demand-state posterior forecast better: shared-minus-private absolute forecast error falls monotonically with adopters (−0.037 at 2 adopters → −0.165 at 8). The audit is the finding. **Under discount-only**, better forecasts flow to consumers: prices fall (−$0.26 at 8 adopters) and consumer surplus *rises* (+$3.59/day) — PASS. **Under rationing** (unconstrained yield management — a believed-busy day *raises* price), the same shared posterior turns collusive: prices rise (+$0.15) and consumer surplus *collapses* (−$8.36/day) — FAIL. **Discount-only is therefore the load-bearing pro-consumer condition** (on this simulation's evidence) — the analytic line between a shared-data cooperative and RealPage. A companion antagonism test (`block/results-b7.json`) shows the block's gains are dominated by own-surface adoption (+$197.02/day at the bodega), not transfer from neighbors (−$23.82/day), so merchant and shopper interests are not structurally opposed. **The coordination channel is mechanized directly (`block/results-b6-bundles.json`).** A slack-gated cross-venue *parking-validation* bundle (a retail sale + validated parking, the culturally pre-accepted case) is a **Pareto win** — +$1,129/day (boutique), +$304/day (eatery), nobody displaced — that *rides slack*, shrinking to zero as the lot fills because it monetizes idle slots, not free money. The pre-registered anti-lever confirms the boundary: an *ungated* validation cannibalizes paying, inelastic commuters and turns a thin-margin venue joint-negative (−$885/day); the slack gate is what keeps it pro-surplus. A second bundle — slack-swap **cross-clearing** of would-spoil stock between complementary venues — grows joint surplus at every excess level (+$22→+$85/day, money and units conserved to <1e-6): the flywheel's durable coordination force reduced to its mechanism.

**The toxic-human-negotiation baseline (`wholesale/results-human.json`).** Against the eight bad-human types, both-sides SNHP dominates on all four failure modes: it **cuts impasse 37.5pp** (the Myerson–Satterthwaite deadweight, $53.60/impasse, ≫ the buffer), **protects the naive +$15.20**, **captures the logroll +$53.60** — where **price-only positional haggling creates $0.00 of joint surplus** (the multi-issue thesis measured: the entire pie is window×qty×terms×spoilage, and pure price-haggling forfeits all of it) — and **lifts relationship LTV +$295.61**. All eight types are better off switching; the hardballer's gain arrives only because a mixed population makes its aggression self-defeating (its fleecing edge over the naive drops from ~$50 to ~$27).

### 6. Related work

**Posted-price optimality and bargaining.** Riley & Zeckhauser (1983) — a committed seller with known demand cannot be beaten by haggling; we replicate it as the control (§5a) and treat any "win" there as a bug. Distortion in bargaining under private information (Crawford & Varian, 1979; Sobel, 1981) predicts misreporting pays — which we find *only* where the seller's reservation is report-dependent (§3, §5c).

**Bargaining, arbitration, and the outside-option principle.** The outcome rule — quote the (asymmetric) Nash split of the gains above a disagreement point — is the *Nash-in-Nash* pricing object of applied bilateral-oligopoly work (Collard-Wexler, Gowrisankaran & Lee, 2019) and the Nash-as-arbitration device (Yildiz, 2011); we disclaim the 1950 axioms (§2) and inherit this lineage rather than claim it. The event-consistent disagreement point that carries our emergent-IC result *is* the **outside-option principle** (Binmore, Rubinstein & Wolinsky, 1986; Binmore, Shaked & Sutton, 1989): the operative threat is the *max* of the inside continuation and the outside option. Our one design increment is that the *same* disclosed valuation sets both the trade surplus and the buyer's threat, so a price-lowering understatement is self-canceling; the general statement that an ex-post-efficient outcome cannot depend on a privately-reported disagreement point is van Damme & Lang (2024, arXiv:2211.06830) — whose Theorem 1 (linear frontier, any correlation) covers our *single-issue* case exactly, which is why the single-unit proof goes through, and which stops covering us the moment the frontier is nonlinear (multi-issue) — precisely where our open theory frontier lies (§8).

**Impossibility and the frontier.** Myerson & Satterthwaite (1983) — no efficient, IR, budget-balanced, IC bilateral mechanism under two-sided uncertainty. Chatterjee & Samuelson (1983) — the no-trade interval, to which the min-gain buffer is *loosely analogous* (their band is an endogenous equilibrium region from two-sided uncertainty; ours an exogenous design deadband). The impossibility of truthful fixed-price bilateral trade (Segal-Halevi & Hassidim, arXiv:1711.08057 — no truthful mechanism guarantees more than 1/M of the optimal gains-from-trade with M goods) and the self-interested-mediator trilemma (Fan et al., arXiv:2410.11683 — a revenue-seeking mediator cannot jointly attain IC, obedience, and informativeness) bound the bilateral-trade frontier from the impossibility side; SNHP is a constructive, *neutral* (zero-cut) point measured against optimized-posted baselines, not a rent-extracting mediator (§3). The min-gain buffer is, concretely, a Myerson (1981) optimal-reserve / no-trade band — the same excluded-marginal-trade device that makes the dominant-strategy double auction of McAfee (1992; Hagerty & Rogerson, 1987) IR and budget-balanced.

**Mispricing in the field.** DellaVigna & Gentzkow (QJE 2019) — the median chain forgoes ~$16M/year by pricing near-uniformly across stores *despite* being able to estimate store-level demand variation (managerial inertia, brand/fairness-image): a documented failure to price to *known* heterogeneity, and the empirical warrant for the miscalibration channel (§5b).

**Information, price discrimination, and welfare.** Our headline welfare result — the mechanism ties on producer surplus and the disclosure value accrues to consumers (§5a) — is the consumer-surplus vertex of **Bergemann, Brooks & Morris (2015, AER)**: with richer buyer information, producer surplus can be held at the uniform-price level while consumers capture the residual of efficient trade. The constructive route is also known: Kuang, Li, Liu & Yu (2026, arXiv:2603.20609) prove that consumer-IC (voluntary, strategy-proof) segmentation pins producer surplus at the uniform-monopoly profit and *derives* discount-only (no price above the uniform-monopoly price); SNHP is a specific individualized Nash-bargaining *implementation* of that characterization, not the characterization. The pro-consumer/positive-sum data exchange (§5f, the calibration market) is Pareto-improving data-sharing (Gradwohl & Tennenholtz, 2022) and the markets-for-information line (Bergemann & Bonatti, 2019) — indiscriminate sharing would instead erode profit via competition, which is why the exchange is targeted and discount-only-gated; third-degree-PD welfare (Aguirre, Cowan & Vickers, 2010) sets when discrimination raises or lowers total output.

**Revenue management and strategic consumers.** Gallego & van Ryzin (1994; our `gvr` arm); Aviv & Pazgal (2008); Cachon & Swinney (2009); Caro & Gallien (2012, the fashion markdown anchor, §5e). **Kuo, Ahn & Aydın (2011)** is the direct antecedent of our §2 setting — a posted price that is at once the take-it-or-leave-it price and the ceiling from which haggle-prone buyers negotiate *down*, over limited inventory; our delta is the two-sided-disclosure Nash rule for the discount (theirs is sequential/random bargaining), the type-enforced ceiling, and the neutral broker.

**Algorithmic pricing, collusion, and privacy.** Calvano et al. (2020); Fish, Gonczarowski & Shorrer (arXiv:2404.00806) on LLM-pricer collusive drift; and the RealPage line — where the actual DOJ remedy is an *input* restriction (own-data-only), whereas our discount-only-vs-rationing line is an asymmetric price cap, a remedy framing we did not find in prior work — motivating the §5f audit. On the buyer side, keeping WTP on-device reframes surveillance pricing (FTC 6(b) study, 2024–25) as buyer leverage; the economics of privacy and personalized pricing are Acquisti, Taylor & Wagman (2016) and Chen et al. (2022, privacy-preserving dynamic pricing), with the honest caveat that WTP can leak from the interaction itself — the "signed-cart-only" architecture, not an economic novelty, is what bounds it.

**Behavioral pricing fairness.** Kahneman, Knetsch & Thaler (1986) dual entitlement; the ~2023 large-N replication finding <50% unfair (favorable to our conservative churn penalties); category-scoped dynamic-pricing acceptance (restaurants ~52% equate it with gouging) motivating the category-scoped discount-only invariant (§5d, `CALIBRATION-TARGETS.md §5`).

**LLM agent economies and benchmarks.** Vending-Bench (arXiv:2502.15840); Anthropic Project Vend; Magentic Marketplace (arXiv:2510.25779); TERMS-Bench; *The Automated but Risky Game* (arXiv:2506.00073). **CoffeeBench (Sugiura et al., SakanaAI; arXiv:2606.16613; FAGEN @ ICML 2026)** is the closest recent work in *setting* — a multi-firm shop economy (two farmers, two roasters, two retailers over a 90-day horizon) where agents "communicate, negotiate, and transact." It differs from us on the object of study: **CoffeeBench benchmarks LLM-agent *capability*** — whether a given model directs its tool use toward profit-relevant actions, and it documents long-horizon failure modes (e.g. an idle-drift mode) — placing one LLM firm against fixed reference agents. It is not a mechanism-design result: it does not hold the market rule fixed and vary information structure, it does not compare a neutral mechanism against optimized-posted baselines, and it does not carry a theoretical placement or a pre-registered paired-counterfactual battery. **SNHP evaluates a *mechanism*** — a neutral broker specified as a revelation rule — with pre-registered paired simulations, a misreporting battery, and a Myerson–Satterthwaite placement; the LLM is removed from price formation entirely (§5b shows why).

### 7. Limitations

- **The M-S proposition is now proven for the single unit, and refined.** Emergent buyer-IC is proved type-by-type in a stylized single-unit, single-good model (`paper/THEOREM-IC.md`), and the proof *corrects* the earlier four-condition statement: the four are necessary but not sufficient, and closing the excess-unit leak additionally requires **(a′) `ℓ − c_eff ≤ 2β`** (the buffer dominates the excess rent). Two honest caveats on the proof: the IC it delivers is *weak* (in the safe region every report yields the same surplus — honesty is un-rewarded, not strictly rewarded), and the *necessity* of (a)–(c) is shown by construction plus cross-venue illustration (the boba collapse, the naive-disagreement prototype) rather than by within-model counterexamples — only (d) and (a′) have clean in-model counterexamples; a fully in-model necessity proof is future work. The state-conditioned adaptive, oracle-excess, and per-SKU deviations the pooled sweep could not see are now tested (`vend/battery.py`, `boba/battery.py`): honesty survives them on the WTP channel *up to* a bounded excess-unit residual (+$0.15–0.20/day at the sup over types, on high-rent SKUs where (a′) fails), which the pooled mean hid and an MDE analysis quantifies. Still open: the **multi-unit/multi-good** generalization of (a′) (the single-unit bound is O($0.15/day) and the sim confirms it does not blow up, but a tight multi-good statement is future work), and **colluding / dynamic multi-visit** deviations, which remain untested.
- **The theoretical content is a re-combination of known results, not new bargaining theory.** As §1 and §6 now make explicit, the outcome rule is Nash-in-Nash/arbitration, the disagreement structure is the outside-option principle, the buffer is a reserve/no-trade band, the M-S placement is that theorem's own two-sided hypothesis, and the §5a welfare split is the Bergemann–Brooks–Morris consumer-surplus vertex. The increment is *constructive and empirical* — the self-canceling disagreement coupling, the deployable discount-only/identity-blind packaging, the neutral price-forming A2A broker, the discount-only collusion line, and the pre-registered evidence program — and the paper should be read as a mechanism-with-evidence, not a theory contribution.
- **The fairness model is transitional, not A2A.** Its reference-price, loss-aversion, and churn parameters are literature-anchored but not estimated from human data; the quote-salience channel is a design hypothesis a human-subjects study must test. The surge results (§5d) refute a thesis but do not substitute for behavioral measurement.
- **Thin-data single-buy venues.** Vintage and fashion are single-buy seasons where an ~8% estimate error makes concave-margin optimizers lose to simple schedules (§5e); this is a genuine boundary on the mechanism's estimate-hungry variants, not a universal claim.
- **Per-machine vs route economics.** Vending dollars are per-machine and thin (7–8 vends/day); the commercial unit is the route, and route-level figures are scaled, not directly simulated.
- **Calibration is to public benchmarks, not proprietary data.** Every consumer-model parameter is anchored to a named public source, swept over its evidence band, or labeled an assumption (`CALIBRATION-TARGETS.md`, `NEIGHBORHOOD-CALIBRATION.md`): vending traffic to SOTI/Cantaloupe 2025 (and a public Kaggle NJ-vending fit target); resale hazard to ThredUp FY2025; eBay Best-Offer dynamics to Backus et al. (QJE 2020); boba balking to Lu et al. (2013); barber utilization/no-show to Squire/Zenoti; parking elasticity to SFpark; fashion returns to NRF 2024. A physical pilot and a fit to a public transaction corpus are planned, not done. More fundamentally, *every* headline effect is measured inside our own simulator; the program's own meta-pattern (§4) — that each defect we found was an objective term still assuming the pre-mechanism world — is itself a caution that no single self-built simulator's calibration should be taken as *establishing* a mechanism's superiority. External validation (a physical pilot, a public-corpus fit, or a human-subjects salience test) is the load-bearing next step, not a footnote.
- **Single-venue vs endogenous competition.** Most results are single-venue; the block composes ten venues but the competitor's board is still largely exogenous. A fully endogenous two-sided market (buyer brokers vs seller brokers, and whether neutrality is an equilibrium) is future work.

### 8. Future work

**The multi-issue generalization of the emergent-IC characterization is the genuinely open theory frontier — and our actual go-to-market.** van Damme & Lang (2024) prove disagreement-independence only under a *linear* frontier (their Thm 1), ≤2 types, or *independent* disagreement points, with a three-type counterexample where nonlinear-frontier type-dependence is possible and the continuum case left as their own open conjecture. SNHP's multi-issue logrolling breaks all three sufficient conditions at once — a *concave* logroll frontier, an event-*coupled* (correlated) disagreement, and a *continuum* of types — so whether weak emergent IC survives, and the multi-good buffer condition Σᵢ(ℓ−cᵢ) ≤ 2β, is open. It is more tractable than v-D-L's general conjecture (SNHP is one specific *inefficient* rule — the buffer breaks efficiency, so their efficient-mechanism theorems do not bind it — with β a free design knob), and a 2-good concave-frontier extension of the `THEOREM-IC` machinery is the concrete first step. Also on the list: calibration to public vending/retail corpora and a physical pilot; ≥10-seed-family robustness grids with power analysis; adaptive and colluding attack search; the two-sided endogenous case and whether neutrality is an equilibrium; a human-subjects test of quote salience; deposit/subscription terms as bundle issues at appointment venues (the barber's incumbent mechanism); and a rent-aware block accounting so per-venue deltas read against real occupancy cost.
